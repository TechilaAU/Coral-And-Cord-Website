Our Story

Born on the Great Barrier Reef.

Coral & Cord started the way most good ideas do in North Queensland - on the water, a long way from a power point, wondering why fishing shirts had to look the way they did.

We grew up fishing the reefs off the Queensland coast. The water out there does not look like anything printed on a fishing shirt. It looks like coral gardens and tide lines, mangrove roots and bait balls, the slow geometry that builds a reef over ten thousand years. So we decided to put the real thing on the shirt.

Every Coral & Cord pattern begins underwater. We work from photographs and dive notes taken on the reefs we actually fish, then redraw them by hand as fine line work - part scientific illustration, part traditional mandala geometry. The result is apparel that reads as artwork at arm's length and as pure reef up close.

The performance side is non-negotiable. UPF 50+ fabric, moisture wicking, quick dry, flatlock seams. Gear that works 40 nautical miles offshore and still looks right at the brewery on the way home.

From Reef to Garment

How a pattern is made.

Three steps, no shortcuts. Each collection is drawn from a single reef system and carries its story.

01

Dive it

We start in the water - photographing coral structure, fish, current lines and the colours of the reef we are naming the collection after.

02

Draw it

Back on land it becomes fine line work - marine life and reef geometry redrawn by hand into a single repeating artwork.

03

Wear it

The artwork is printed on UPF 50+ technical fabric and cut into shirts, hoodies, shorts and buffs built for long days on the water.

Sustainability

The reef is the whole point.

A brand built on the Great Barrier Reef does not get to treat it as a backdrop. We keep our footprint small and our commitments practical.

  • Recycled and recyclable packaging on every order
  • Print on demand production to avoid dead stock
  • A share of every Reef Collection release supports reef conservation
  • Designed in Australia, made to last more than one season
